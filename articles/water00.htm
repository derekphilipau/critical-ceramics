REPORTS FROM
THE WATERSHED CENTER FOR THE CERAMIC ARTS

During the summer of 2000, Critical Ceramics was given a studio space at the Watershed Center for the Ceramic Arts located in Newcastle, Maine. Editor Forrest Snyder was close by to record the discussions, creations, and going ons of contemporary ceramic artists working in a concentrated studio time.

As participants quickly learn, the Watershed experience encourages the exchange of ideas, group collaboration, material experimentation, and self-inquiry. Aside from some very basic ground rules regarding safety and communial living, each session at Watershed begins with a clean slate. Each artist is free to work and live as he or she chooses. There are few, if any, hard and fast rules. This unrestricted creative environment is unique and special to Watershed. Indeed, it is the basis for its success.

Along with almost unlimited creative freedom, Watershed sets a standard of a non-heirarchical environment within which to work. Artist new and old work side by side, exchanging ideas and methods. All are teachers. All are students.

During the summer, Watershed has several types of sessions. Some are held with an artist invited to attend by Watershed, while other sessions are set up by the artists themselves. An Artist Invites Artists group is formed when one artist invites up to thirteen other artists to work together during one of the summer sessions. This program is a way for professional artists to arrange a residency with their peers.

Critical Ceramics would like to thank all of this summer's artists, Executive Director Lynn Thompson, the Board of Directors, and all of the great staff.
