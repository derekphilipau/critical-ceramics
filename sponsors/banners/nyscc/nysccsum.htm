Hot House Floriculture 2:
Ceramic Sculpture Work Session

Walter McConnell with guest artists
Xavier Toubes, Lu Bin, Jason Green, & Fred Wilson

May 21 through June 15


A hot house is an artificially controlled environment to speed the propagation of plants. This summer session in ceramics at Alfred is an intensive studio experience to speed the propa gation of your work and ideas. Hot House Floriculture 2 will build on the success of last year's session by providing intermediate and advanced students time and space to pursue self-directed projects in sculpture or installation. Walter McConnell, Alfred Associate Professor of Ceramic Art, and visiting artists, Xavier Toubes, Professor of Ceramics at the School of the Art Institute of Chicago and former Artistic Director of the European Ceramic Work Center; Lu Bin, Ceramic Sculptor from China and winner of the Silver Medal at the 52nd Fienza International Ceramic Art Exhibition; and Jason Green, Sculptor and Installation artist, MFA Alfred 1997 will be "in residence" working alongside participants in an open studio situation. Fred Wilson, installation artist and recent MacArthur fellow, well known world wide for rearranging the contents of museum collections, will be in residence for one week working on an installation project to be exhibited at The Fosdick Nelson Gallery at Alfred in 2002.

Our common goal: compress an extraordinary amount of work and critical dialogue into a short amount of time so that the studio becomes a kind of hot house environment where new work and ideas take root and flourish. Technical support provided by Alfred MFA students in kiln firing, moldmaking and casting; slide lectures and discussion by faculty and a guest artist will regularly punctuate the studio experience.


2001 CERAMIC CIRCUS

John Gill

June 25 through July 20


Alfred's Professor of Ceramic Art, John Gill, will lead this summer school program into its second century of excellence. Open to all levels of expertise, this course will explore moldmaking, throwing, handbuilding, sculpture and glaze calculation. The goal is to expand the vocabulary of clay fabrication. Joining Professor Gill will be some of our leading ceramic artists. They will work closely with all summer school students, as well as present their own work and techniques. Additionally, this program will include the assistance of Alfred's graduate MFA students, as well as lectures and discussions by Alfred's full-time ceramic faculty. Work in Alfred, engage the artists, and utilize our facilities, from kilns and library to museum. See your ideas come to life.
