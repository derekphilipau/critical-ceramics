SUMMER RESIDENCY

Watershed Center for the Ceramic Arts is a nonprofit residency program for artists from across the country and abroad. The peaceful environment and informal atmosphere provide residents with undisturbed time to concentrate on their work. The Watershed experience encourages the exchange of ideas, collaboration, experimentation, exploration, and self-inquiry . Up to twenty artists per session work, eat, and live in the intimate community setting.

OPPORTUNITIES

Participants: Any serious artist may come to Watershed for a residency. All sessions are open to participants. Priority is given to early applicants.

Artists Invite Artists: An "AIA" group is formed when one artist invites up to thirteen other artists to work together during one of the summer sessions. This program is a way for professional artists to arrange a residency with their peers. Artists who work in any media are welcome. Additional artists may join a previously formed AIA session simply by applying for that session.

Fully and Partially Funded Residency Awards: Fully funded residents are given room, meals, and studio free of charge. There are also partially funded residencies available. All artists are selected by a review committee. Residency awards are contingent upon funding.

Guest Artists: Each summer Watershed invites one or two established or emerging artists to come and work in the studio. Guest Artists do not teach, but are integral members of a residency group.

Assistantships: Summer assistantships are awarded to applicants in need of financial aid. The residency fee is reduced in exchange for work hours.

Studio Rental: Studio space is available for participants who choose to secure living accommodations locally. Space may be rented by the session or by the month.

Winter Residency: Four artists participate in a nine month residency from September through May. Each artist receives living accomodation and studio space.

FACILITY

The studio is housed in a spacious old brickworks barn. Sixteen thousand square feet of space on two floors supply ample, flexible studios. Equipment includes two 60 cubic foot low-fire propane car kilns, a 30 cubic foot high fire propane kiln, a 30 cubic foot wood kiln, various low and high-fire electric kilns, potters wheels, a Soldner clay mixer, and a Walker pug mill. A hillside of local earthenware brick clay is free and abundant. Other standard clay and glaze materials are available for purchase. Studio technicians manage and maintain the studio.

A separate residence building provides dorm housing for residents. A central living area and dining room are the sites for conversation and superb, healthy meals prepared by staff artists.

LOCATION

Located in Newcastle, Maine, Watershed is about an hour's drive northeast of Portland and three hours from Boston. The thirty-two rural acres are set on gently rolling hills of both open and wooded land, surrounded by a neighboring farm, nature conservancy land, and the Sheepscot River. Several lakes and the ocean are a short drive away.

The mission of Watershed Center for the Ceramic Arts is to provide serious artists with time and space to create in clay.
