shane posted 10-04-2001 09:39 AM ET (US)        
>"JG: I�m really, really, really tired of black lines on orange flashing. Across the board. Woodfire, that stripe/flash is kind of the raku of the late 1990�s. The Ruggles and Rankin style has consumed our field! Don�t get me wrong, they make some lovely pots; the problem is, now lots of other people make lovely Ruggles and Rankin pots, too."
I have to agree that it is quite disturbing, the number of people making rock creek pots is at best growing old, But we do learn from our mentors and douglas and will have given alot to the overall picture of ceramics history so far!(theyre still growing in their own work) The thing is you can't critisize someones infections, their work being copied is a direct result of the number of workshops they have done. I must point out that julia is now doing her large share of workshops. Already in undergrad and grad studios we are seeing pots that look alot like julia's. I am not saying these are bad pots or that this is a bad thing, I just don't believe it is fair to judge their influence on woodfiring. Soda firing is now the trend, and everyone will hop on the wagon. The orange flash is a formula that works in woodfiring, what will be the dominate flavor in the soda atmosphere? maybe cone 6 with lusters? (although matt longs use of carbon trapping on flashing slip seems to be in the lead)We will not know for a few more years. I think it is okay to be influenced by someone's style or methods, but we must be careful on how closely we work with those styles or methods. Julia has a very unique style and method, this sets it up to be admired and copied. Lets not shoot the courrier for delivering this lovely pallete of color for woodfiring.

yours
shane
