bill posted 23-07-2003 04:18 PM ET (US)            
Getting back to the initial question, theories help develop understanding. This holds true for ceramics. As understanding of the medium itself developes so should the theory surrounding it. Although form and technique is also important, it is separate from theory - one can learn to throw and fire a form but never consider how other cultures (for example) have approached that same form or process. As a result, I do think that there is real value in a craft theory. I also believe that there is corresponding value to learning from art theory (regardless of whether or not the craft and art have the same "root"). Expression through the medium of clay need not be a "bastard step-child" nor divorsed from the larger craft or art worlds. However, ceramics does require it's own understanding - its own language - and therefore its own theory. I look forward to continueing this discussion.
