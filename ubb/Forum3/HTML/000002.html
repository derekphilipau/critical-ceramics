clayjeff posted 10-04-2003 10:19 AM ET (US)            
I think the surface treatment should be a natural one applied over time. Fire the clay body to the color you desire cone 04 for example and then set the work outside in a "cemetery garden" to get that natural patina. Whether it is an urban or rural setting the forces of the environment can provide an interesting surface. It is not a quick finish, but could be a satisfying surface.

Sincerely, Jeff

Cone10 posted 10-04-2003 10:36 PM ET (US)            
Intriguing work and intriguing problem. Given that you want these pieces to remind the viewer of something or someplace, I think each finish needs to make a unique contribution to the rest of the story. What was the path like that led to their discoveries? What was the general condition of the cemetary... how were they sited on the landscape, how were they maintained,were there any offerings, has the surrounding area changed with the passage of time? Have there been wars, floods, vandals... Perhaps thinking about these and similar questions will help with your continued explorations.
