gulden posted 14-06-2004 10:34 PM ET (US)            
Jennifer,

Your question merits a significant amount of research on your part. Location, faculty, cost/aid, past graduates, program stability and support, size, trend toward a type of work all should be considered in your search. Grad school should not be taken lightly - nor should it be your magnum opus - but with a bit of earnest evaluation of your wants and needs, and phone call to prospective faculty and their current/past students you can narrow the list down pretty quickly.
Also, you might find taking more than a year off beneficial to your mind-set when looking at schools. If you've never taken time off during/between high school and BFA there is a lot you are missing in terms of learning about yourself being an artist and what that entails. A residency program may give you some time to address some of these demons, or perhaps simply investing your time in a studio outside the institution would be best.

I wish you the best,

Tyler
