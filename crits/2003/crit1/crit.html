Forrest Snyder

In the summer of 2002, I began exploring old Vermont cemetaries with a pinhole camera. What has drawn me to headstones over the years have been the stories they told, or more precisely, the stories I imagined they told. Returning to the studio, I have begun creating lifesize headstones for fictional characters. I want them to remind the viewer of something or someplace other. These 4 stones are uncompleted. While I'm clear about some of what I'd like, I am baffled by other aspects, mainly related to surface treatement which leads me to think about intent - how would these stones be different if glazed with a drippy, glossy, rainbow than if glazed with matte glazes looking like real rock? How is the narrative changed with different surface treatments? What could be lost? Gained?
